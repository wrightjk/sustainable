Sustainable Living While Traveling

1

Pack your own toiletries

Bring your own toiletries to avoid using one-time-use toiletries commonly used in hotels. Some hotels have switched from these single-use toiletries in favor of dispensers, which could be an alternative option.

2

Fly less

If possible for your destination, avoid flying. Air travel is one of the fastest growing contributors to greenhouse gas emissions. If you do fly, try to take a direct flight if able. Taking off and landing contribute to 25% of airplane emissions.

3

Clean up after yourself

If you’ve ever seen a beach after students’ spring break, you’ll know what I’m talking about. Leave the places you go better than you found them.

4

Refuse single-use laundering

Hotels often launder towels and bedsheets after one use. Some hotels offer signs to let hotel staff know you aren’t interested in single-use laundering. Laundry contributes to 16% of a hotel’s water usage.

5

Support the local economy

Meet real community members in your travel destination by supporting their shops and contributing to the local economy. This cuts down on commercial goods production and transportation and provides jobs for the locals.

6

Don’t buy wildlife souvenirs

Buying wildlife souvenirs is buying into a marketplace of trafficked wildlife products, sometimes rare or endangered wildlife products.

7

Use reef-safe sunscreen

Avoid Oxybenzone-based sunscreens, which damage our reefs and aquatic life, and instead opt for products containing zinc.

8

Offset your journey

Offset your travels by buying credits towards an environmentally-friendly project, such as avoiding emission of greenhouse gases or sequestering carbon dioxide. Just remember to do your research on offsets as they tend to be unregulated.

9

Consider a capsule wardrobe

Packing lighter can reduce your overall emissions footprint no matter your travel method.

10

Prepare for your absence

Even if devices are turned off, they still draw energy while plugged in. Make sure to unplug your electronics and appliances not in use, adjust your thermostat, and consider suspending subscription services while you’re away.
